# Logical Reasoning Knowledge Base for AAIPL

## Topic 1: Syllogisms

### Core Concepts
A syllogism is a logical argument with two premises and a conclusion.

**Structure:**
- Major Premise: All A are B
- Minor Premise: All C are A  
- Conclusion: Therefore, all C are B

**Types of Syllogisms:**

1. **Categorical Syllogisms**
   - All A are B (Universal Affirmative)
   - No A are B (Universal Negative)
   - Some A are B (Particular Affirmative)
   - Some A are not B (Particular Negative)

2. **Valid Forms:**
   - Barbara: All M are P, All S are M → All S are P
   - Celarent: No M are P, All S are M → No S are P
   - Darii: All M are P, Some S are M → Some S are P
   - Ferio: No M are P, Some S are M → Some S are not P

3. **Common Fallacies:**
   - Undistributed Middle: Middle term not distributed in either premise
   - Illicit Major/Minor: Terms distributed in conclusion but not in premises
   - Exclusive Premises: Both premises are negative
   - Affirmative Conclusion from Negative Premise

**Examples:**

Valid:
- All humans are mortal
- Socrates is human
- Therefore, Socrates is mortal

Invalid:
- All cats are animals
- All dogs are animals
- Therefore, all cats are dogs (undistributed middle)

### Key Rules:
1. Middle term must be distributed at least once
2. If a term is distributed in conclusion, it must be distributed in premise
3. From two negative premises, no conclusion follows
4. If one premise is negative, conclusion must be negative
5. From two particular premises, no conclusion follows

## Topic 2: Seating Arrangements (Linear, Circular)

### Linear Arrangements

**Key Concepts:**
- Fixed positions vs relative positions
- Left/Right orientation matters
- End positions have special properties

**Common Patterns:**

1. **Direct Information:**
   - A sits at position 3
   - B sits to the left of C
   - D sits between E and F

2. **Relative Information:**
   - A sits two places from B
   - C sits at one end
   - D is not adjacent to E

3. **Conditional Information:**
   - If A sits at position 1, then B sits at position 5
   - Either C or D sits in the middle

**Solving Strategy:**
1. Draw a linear arrangement (1-2-3-4-5-6)
2. Mark fixed positions first
3. Use relative positions to constrain possibilities
4. Eliminate contradictions
5. Verify all conditions

### Circular Arrangements

**Key Differences:**
- No fixed start/end points
- Clockwise vs counterclockwise matters
- Opposite positions are n/2 apart (for n people)
- Adjacent means next to, not left/right specifically

**Important Rules:**
- n people can be arranged in (n-1)! ways in a circle
- If clockwise/counterclockwise are same, then (n-1)!/2 ways

**Common Question Types:**

1. **Fixed Reference:**
   - A sits at 12 o'clock position
   - B sits opposite to A
   - C sits to the immediate right of A

2. **Relative Positions:**
   - A and B sit opposite each other
   - C sits between D and E
   - F is two positions clockwise from G

3. **Directional:**
   - All face center vs All face outward
   - Clockwise vs Counterclockwise

**Solving Strategy:**
1. Draw a circle with n positions
2. Fix one person as reference (eliminates rotation)
3. Mark opposite/adjacent positions
4. Use clockwise/counterclockwise constraints
5. Verify all face same/different direction

## Topic 3: Blood Relations and Family Tree

### Core Relationships

**Generation 1 (Same Level):**
- Brother, Sister, Cousin
- Husband, Wife
- Brother-in-law, Sister-in-law

**Generation +1 (One Up):**
- Father, Mother
- Uncle (father's/mother's brother)
- Aunt (father's/mother's sister)
- Father-in-law, Mother-in-law

**Generation +2 (Two Up):**
- Grandfather, Grandmother
- Great-uncle, Great-aunt

**Generation -1 (One Down):**
- Son, Daughter
- Nephew (brother's/sister's son)
- Niece (brother's/sister's daughter)
- Son-in-law, Daughter-in-law

**Generation -2 (Two Down):**
- Grandson, Granddaughter

### Key Rules:

1. **Gender Determination:**
   - Brother's/Father's/Son's/Uncle's → Male
   - Sister's/Mother's/Daughter's/Aunt's → Female

2. **Relationship Chains:**
   - Father's father = Grandfather
   - Mother's brother = Maternal uncle
   - Sister's son = Nephew
   - Brother's wife = Sister-in-law

3. **In-law Relationships:**
   - Spouse's sibling = Brother/Sister-in-law
   - Spouse's parent = Father/Mother-in-law
   - Sibling's spouse = Brother/Sister-in-law
   - Child's spouse = Son/Daughter-in-law

4. **Special Cases:**
   - Only son/daughter (no siblings)
   - Only child (no brothers or sisters)
   - Step-relations (remarriage)
   - Adopted relations

### Common Question Patterns:

**Pattern 1: Direct Chain**
"A's father's sister's son" = A's cousin

**Pattern 2: Gender Puzzle**
"A said to B: Your mother is the only daughter of my mother"
Analysis: A's mother's only daughter = A (if female) or A's sister
Answer: B is A's son/daughter (or nephew/niece)

**Pattern 3: Pointing Photo**
"A man pointing to a photo says: His mother is the wife of my father"
Analysis: Wife of my father = My mother
His mother = My mother → He is my brother
Answer: Brother

**Pattern 4: Complex Chain**
"If A is B's father and C's son, what is C to B?"
Analysis: A is C's son means C is A's parent
A is B's father means B is A's child
Therefore C is B's grandparent

### Solving Strategy:
1. Draw a family tree
2. Mark male (□) and female (○) symbols
3. Connect with lines (= for marriage, | for parent-child)
4. Trace the relationship path
5. Count generations and determine relation

## Topic 4: Series and Patterns (Alphanumeric)

### Number Series Patterns

**Common Types:**

1. **Arithmetic Progression (AP):**
   - Constant difference: 2, 5, 8, 11, 14 (+3)
   - Double difference: 1, 2, 4, 7, 11, 16 (+1, +2, +3, +4, +5)

2. **Geometric Progression (GP):**
   - Constant ratio: 2, 6, 18, 54, 162 (×3)
   - Square/Cube: 1, 4, 9, 16, 25 (1², 2², 3², 4², 5²)

3. **Fibonacci Type:**
   - Each term = sum of previous two: 1, 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13
   - Modified: 2, 3, 5, 8, 13, 21

4. **Prime Number Series:**
   - 2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13, 17, 19, 23

5. **Alternating Series:**
   - Two patterns interleaved: 2, 10, 4, 12, 6, 14, 8
   - (2, 4, 6, 8) and (10, 12, 14)

6. **Mixed Operations:**
   - +2, ×2, +2, ×2: 1, 3, 6, 8, 16, 18
   - n² + n: 2, 6, 12, 20, 30 (1²+1, 2²+2, 3²+3, 4²+4, 5²+5)

### Letter Series Patterns

**Common Types:**

1. **Alphabetical Position:**
   - A=1, B=2, C=3, ... Z=26
   - Skip letters: A, C, E, G, I (+2 positions)

2. **Reverse Order:**
   - Z, Y, X, W, V (-1 position from end)

3. **Mixed Number-Letter:**
   - A1, B2, C3, D4, E5
   - Consonants: B, C, D, F, G, H, J, K, L, M, N
   - Vowels: A, E, I, O, U

4. **Word Patterns:**
   - CAT, DOG, BAT, COW (3-letter animals)
   - First letter alphabetical: APPLE, BALL, CAT, DOG

### Alphanumeric Mixed Series

**Pattern Types:**

1. **Alternating:**
   - A1, B2, C3, D4, E5
   - 1A, 2B, 3C, 4D, 5E

2. **Position-based:**
   - A = 1st letter, 1 = 1st number
   - Z = 26th letter, 26 = number

3. **Operation-based:**
   - A2 (A=1, 1×2=2)
   - B3 (B=2, 2+1=3)
   - C6 (C=3, 3×2=6)

### Solving Strategy:
1. Look for differences between consecutive terms
2. Check for second-order differences
3. Test for arithmetic/geometric progression
4. Check for prime numbers
5. Look for alternating patterns
6. For letters, check alphabetical position
7. Look for skip patterns (every 2nd, 3rd term)

### Common Tricks:
- Missing number is the odd one out
- Pattern changes halfway through
- Two interleaved series
- Reverse pattern at midpoint

## Advanced Problem-Solving Techniques

### General Strategy:

1. **Read Carefully:**
   - Identify the type of problem
   - Note all given information
   - Identify what is being asked

2. **Visualize:**
   - Draw diagrams (family tree, seating, etc.)
   - Use symbols (male/female, positions)
   - Mark known information first

3. **Apply Logic:**
   - Use elimination
   - Test each option
   - Look for contradictions

4. **Verify:**
   - Check answer satisfies all conditions
   - Ensure no logical inconsistencies

### Time-Saving Tips:

1. **Pattern Recognition:**
   - Memorize common syllogism forms
   - Know standard series patterns
   - Remember relationship chains

2. **Elimination:**
   - Remove obviously wrong options first
   - Use constraint violations to eliminate

3. **Shortcut Methods:**
   - For circular: Fix one person, arrange others
   - For linear: Start from fixed positions
   - For series: Check last term first

## Common Error Types to Avoid

1. **Syllogisms:**
   - Don't assume "Some A are B" means "Some A are not B"
   - Don't reverse statements without valid conversion
   - Watch for distributed/undistributed terms

2. **Seating:**
   - Circular: Don't confuse clockwise/counterclockwise
   - Linear: Don't confuse left/right orientation
   - Don't forget facing direction changes relationships

3. **Blood Relations:**
   - Don't confuse in-law relations
   - Don't assume gender without clear indicators
   - Don't skip generations in chains

4. **Series:**
   - Don't assume pattern continues same way
   - Don't miss alternating sequences
   - Don't ignore position values in alphanumeric

## Practice Problem Categories

### Easy (Foundation):
- Simple syllogisms (2 premises, direct)
- Linear seating with 4-5 people
- Direct blood relations (father's brother)
- Simple arithmetic series (+n, ×n)

### Medium (Application):
- Complex syllogisms (3+ statements)
- Circular seating with 6-8 people
- Two-step blood relations (father's sister's son)
- Mixed operation series

### Hard (Analysis):
- Multiple syllogisms with interconnected statements
- Circular seating with conditions and facing directions
- Complex family trees with marriages and generations
- Alphanumeric with multiple pattern layers

### Expert (Synthesis):
- Syllogisms with universal and particular statements
- Combined linear and circular arrangements
- Family puzzles with incomplete information
- Series with changing patterns mid-sequence
